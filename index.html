MFA in Creative Writing. What's next?

If you are pursuing an undergraduate or a graduate degree in Creative Writing from Miami University and looking for job opportunities, this website is for you. Below are a few tools and resources to help you through your career search and preparation.

Career/Major Exploration

  • What Can I Do With a Creative Writing degree?: Explore industries and employers relevant to your Creative Writing degree, as well links to additional resources, including job boards.
  • O*Net Online: A database, containing hundreds of standardized and occupation-specific descriptors. Look up the outlook on the field of Creative Writing.
  • Vault's Career Insider: Vault provides in-depth intelligence on what itâ€™s really like to work in an industry, company, or profession.
  • Candid Career: Candid Career provides honest career information and career planning help through videos of real people speaking about their career experiences.

Resume Resources

  • Resume Webpage: Resumes Webpage is an informational page for whether you are developing your resume from scratch or revising an existing document.
  • VMock: VMock leverages technologies like data science, artificial intelligence, and natural language processing to provide instant personalized feedback on your resume.
  • How to Align Dates on a Resume (Microsoft Word)

Cover Letter Resources

  • Cover Letter Guide: Please review our Cover Letters webpage. This is a resource you can use for several different forms of written communications with employers and organizations. There are examples of cover letters for different industries included.
  • Feedback: Feel free to make an appointment with Center for Career Exploration and Success (CCES) via Handshake to discuss drafts of your cover letter or any other application materials.

Networking and Interviewing

  • Miami AlumniConnect allows you to search for and connect with Miami alumni based on major, industry, and location.
  • LinkedIn is the world's largest professional network, with 774+ million members.
    • How to Search for Alumni on LinkedIn

  • Handshake: A database of full-time, part-time, co-op/internship, and non-profit positions filtered and maintained by the Center for Career Exploration & Success and Career Services & Professional Development.
  • Big Interview is an interview training website that offers Miami students information on anticipating and answering interview questions, being aware of body language, and numerous other skills important to the interviewing process. You can practice by registering for a live Mock Interview with CCES.

Job/Internship Searching Techniques and Postings

  • Parker Dewey: Micro-Internships: Parker Dewey offers the largest network of highly motivated college students and recent graduates who are excited to complete short-term, paid, professional assignments.
  • Higher Ed. Jobs: HigherEdJobs is the leading source for jobs and career information in academia.
  • CCES Internship Grants: Internship and Research Grants help students offset personal costs affiliated with internship completion.
  • Handshake: How To Search for Jobs and Internships (Video Version)
  • Handshake: How To Create Search Alerts

Events

Miami University's Center for Career Exploration and Success creates multiple opportunities for students to learn more about different careers, connect with employers, and grow skills and expertise. You can visit the Programs and Events page to check out their upcoming programs that may appeal to your career interests.

Do check out the page for Spring and Fall Career Fairs on campus. Participating in these fairs can provide an opportunity to network with diverse companies, present resumes, and build valuable contacts.
